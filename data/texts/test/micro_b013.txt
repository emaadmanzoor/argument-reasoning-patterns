Should public health insurance cover treatments in complementary and alternative medicine?

a1: Health insurance companies should naturally cover alternative medical treatments.
a2: Not all practices and approaches that are lumped together under this term may have been proven in clinical trials,
a3: yet it's precisely their positive effect when accompanying conventional 'western' medical therapies that's been demonstrated as beneficial.
a4: Besides many general practitioners offer such counselling and treatments in parallel anyway - and who would want to question their broad expertise?
