Should public health insurance cover treatments in complementary and alternative medicine?

a1: From time immemorial humans have had complementary medicine,
a2: and it has not generally been ineffective.
a3: A reduction in the amount of chemically produced medication per person is most certainly desirable.
a4: This will do little damage to the pharmaceutical industry, and
a5: conventional medicine will also hardly suffer.
a6: The patient could learn to be more aware of his or her body and to treat it more healthily.
