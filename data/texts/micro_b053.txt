Should public health insurance cover treatments in complementary and alternative medicine?

a1: Many people see proven relief of their symptoms and complaints by complementary medicine.
a2: However there is no substantiated data that this healing isn't simply due to the placebo effect.
a3: Besides many practices in this field are not regulated professions,
a4: which means that quacks and phonies can practice these occupations unknown to the patients.
a5: That's why the statutory health insurance companies should not cover such treatments.
a6: It would be conceivable to invest more into the training and control of this occupation sector on the part of the state.
